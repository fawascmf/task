THE LIBRARY

An electronic library – an e.Lib – of associated
sites with a foundation based in Anthroposophy:
our book publishing wing, Anthroposophical
Publications, On-line Study Groups, Fine Art
Presentations, an e.Gallery, the KnowNews
NewsWire, "Now I See ..." book reviews, and
several varied bLogs. Be sure to
scroll down to access these links.

Founded in October of 1980, we have provided
Anthroposophically-oriented content to the world
at no charge since then ... in the spirit of the
original Internet!

                                                                                            


     

initiatives of the e.Lib survive on small donations, and an annual grant.
We're a small not-for-profit trying to cover the cost of bandwidth, servers, and
equipment. If we are useful for you, please consider helping. Thank you very
much.


 

PLEASE VISIT OUR ANTHROPOSOPHICAL WEBSITES:


  1. Anthroposophical Publications -- A platform for Anthroposophically-inspired writers to publish their works at cost or completely free, and
    especially translating into English and publishing the over 3,000 untranslated lectures of the Austrian philosopher, Rudolf Steiner [1861-1925].
    We feel that if it's available in German, it should be available in English! Steiner himself said, “and English is a universal world language, and
    will become so more and more.” (from The Kingdom of Childhood lecture series, lecture on August 12th 1924).
  2. Fine Art Presentations -- an e.Gallery with the works of over 650 artists. It is the only site on the Internet featuring Anthroposophically
    -inspired artists as well as the works of artists from classical to modern, Renaissance to Pin-up, from the 13th Century onward, categorized by
    art movement, ethnicity, and with special collections of art. Some artists are linked to Rudolf Steiner's lectures on Art so you can get an
    Anthroposophic perspective of these artist's works.
  3. KnowNews dot Net -- a NewsWire with the latest International news, Sports, Weather, Science, Economics, and uniquely featuring items of
    Anthroposophical interest gathered from worldwide sources. These anthroposophical articles are categorized and searchable, going back
    several years. Biodynamics, Eurythmy, Waldorf Education, Camphill Movement, a large Miscellaneous category, and more.
  4. Now I See ... -- book reviews, written by Anthroposophists, of non-anthroposophical books. And in the spirit of Franz Brentano. Books about
    the World Wars, UFO's, Psychic Detectives, religious artifacts and writings, biographies, and much, much more! You can read about Franz
    Brentano there, too!
